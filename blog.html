Proof of the Generalized Formula for the Union of Subsets

Let \( A_1, A_2, \dots, A_n \) be subsets of a finite set \( S \). We aim to prove the formula for the size of the union:

\( m(A_1 \cup A_2 \cup \cdots \cup A_n) = \sum_{i=1}^n m(A_i) - \sum_{1 \leq i < j \leq n} m(A_i \cap A_j) + \cdots + (-1)^{n+1} m(A_1 \cap A_2 \cap \cdots \cap A_n). \)

Proof

We use the principle of inclusion-exclusion to count the elements in the union \( A_1 \cup A_2 \cup \cdots \cup A_n \).

  1. First, we sum the sizes of all the individual sets \( A_i \): \[ \sum_{i=1}^n m(A_i). \]
  2. Next, we subtract the sizes of all pairwise intersections \( A_i \cap A_j \) (as these are counted twice in the first term): \[ \sum_{1 \leq i < j \leq n} m(A_i \cap A_j). \]
  3. We then add back the sizes of all triple intersections \( A_i \cap A_j \cap A_k \) (as these were subtracted too many times in the previous step): \[ \sum_{1 \leq i < j < k \leq n} m(A_i \cap A_j \cap A_k). \]
  4. This process continues, alternating subtraction and addition, until we reach the intersection of all \( n \) sets: \[ (-1)^{n+1} m(A_1 \cap A_2 \cap \cdots \cap A_n). \]

Hence, the size of the union is given by:

\( m(A_1 \cup A_2 \cup \cdots \cup A_n) = \sum_{i=1}^n m(A_i) - \sum_{1 \leq i < j \leq n} m(A_i \cap A_j) + \cdots + (-1)^{n+1} m(A_1 \cap A_2 \cap \cdots \cap A_n). \)

Conclusion

The principle of inclusion-exclusion ensures that every element in \( S \) is counted exactly once in \( A_1 \cup A_2 \cup \cdots \cup A_n \).
